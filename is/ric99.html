Abstract

                     History and Perspective


   Statistical Mechanics is a traditional subject in Florence, which
has been accounted for by this 15-year old INFN initiative. 
Teams of other cities have joined during the years. 
Most of the people had a field theory background, and thus have been
investigating the fruitful relations and analogies among statistical, 
condensed matter and particle physics.

  In the last few years, a group of people got more deeply involved with 
dynamical systems and chaos, and with applied statistical mechanics; 
these are very interesting subjects, but are not considered among the 
main themes of this founding body; after some discussions last year, 
most of these people left the initiative and joined INFM, a relatively 
newer agency for condensed matter physics. 

  Therefore, this application shows considerable changes.
The teams of all the cities other than Florence have left.
Two new groups of Genova and Trieste have joined.
There is a renewed emphasis on the applications of field theories,
in particular the exactly solvable ones in two dimensions, like
the conformal field theories and the integrable systems.

  The spectrum of research subjects in this initiative is rather broad; 
as a matter of fact, this is a general feature in statistical physics.
Many interesting problems await a decent solution by field theory and 
other theoretical methods: high-temperature superconductivity, quantum
anti-ferromagnets, random and disordered systems, spin glasses, 
self-organized criticality, open systems out of equilibrium.
Conversely, the solution of these phenomena might influence 
theoretical and mathematical physics at large.

FIRENZE FI11/1999 PROGRAM
=========================

- A.Cappelli and F. Colomo
``Study of statistical models with frustration and no quenched disorder''
Try to solve the frustrated XY model; potential applications
of the q-deformed algebras as in the case of the frustrated Spherical model.

- A. Cappelli and G.R. Zemba; A. Cappelli, L. Georgiev and I. Todorov
``Conformal field theories with W-infinity symmetry and the quantum Hall effect''
Explore the physics of the W-infinity minimal models and try to propose an 
experiment.
Study W-infinity symmetry, or relics of it, in the non-Abelian Hall states.

- S. Ruffo, T. Cretegny, T. Dauxois and A. Torcini
``Dynamics of breathers in spin chains''
Exact and numerical study of breather solutions and their stability.

- S. Ruffo, V. Latora and A. Rapisarda
``Dynamics near the phase transition''
Applications to plasma physics and fragmentation of nuclei.

- S. Ruffo, F. Izrailev and L. Tessieri
``Anderson localization''
An original analytic method will be further applied to certain random 
matrix models

- F. Bagnoli, M. Bezzi, P. Palmerini and R. Rechtman
``Cellular Automata and Self-organized Criticality''
Sincronization of extended chaotic systems. Cellular automata and algorithms
for parallel computing.

- S. Marmi, P. Moussa and J.-C. Yoccoz,
``Renormalization and the problem of small divisors''
The (complex) renormalization group applied to some Hamiltonian systems

- S. Marmi and D. Sauzin,
``Asymptotic analysis and Borel summability in the Hamilton-Jacobi equation''
Study of the breaking of the separatrics in the perturbed pendulum and other 
systems.

- S. Marmi and J. Laskar
``Applications of the Laskar method of frequency analysis''
Study other applications of this method, which has been used for celestial 
mechanics and particle dynamics in high-energy accelerators.

- M. Pettini, R. Franzosi, L. Casetti, C. Clementi, G. Pettini and R. Gatto
``Phase transitions and the topology changes in configuration space''
The equipotential sub-manifolds of the configuration space of an Hamiltonian 
system undergo a topology change in correspondence of a phase transition. 
Further studies of this fact, which will apply the Morse theory.

TRIESTE FI11/1999 PROGRAM
=========================

- G. Mussardo and collaborators.
``Supersymmetry in Statistical Models'' 
Exact calculation of the matrix elements and correlators of order parameters in 
statistical models which present supersymmetry in their continuum limit.

``Non-integrable Quantum Field Theory''
Development of new approches to study the spectrum and the dynamics of 
statistical models in a generic point of their phase diagram.

``Random System''
Study Field Theory approaches to random systems.

``Boundary Field Theory'' 
Study of the physical effects in field theories and strongly correlated systems 
induced by the boundary conditions imposed on the order parameters.
